Privacy Policy

This privacy policy explains how PACR processes personal data – both on the public website pacr.app and inside the PACR application behind the login. It sets out which data is retrieved from connected services such as Garmin Connect, Strava or COROS and how you can disconnect these integrations at any time.

2. Scope

This privacy policy applies to:

  • the public landing page at pacr.app,
  • the PACR web application (including the PWA) behind the login,
  • the backend APIs operated by PACR that process training data,
  • data retrieved from connected services such as Garmin Connect, Strava or COROS.

3. Categories of Data We Process

Depending on how you use PACR, we process the following categories of personal data:

  • Account data: e-mail address, password (stored as a BCrypt hash), name, date of birth, sex, maximum heart rate, weight, height, and optional profile fields such as city or region.
  • Training and activity data: uploaded FIT, TCX or GPX files and the metrics derived from them or retrieved via APIs – in particular time, distance, pace, speed, heart rate (average, max, min, zones), power (watts), cadence, elevation, GPS coordinates and device information (manufacturer, model, serial number, software version).
  • Activity streams: second-level time-series data for heart rate, speed, elevation, distance, position, cadence and power.
  • Computed metrics: e.g. VO2max, TRIMP, Training Load, Strain, ACWR, Readiness and body-composition indicators.
  • Community and usage data: feed posts, comments, group and trainer-event participation, community routes (including GPX tracks), leaderboard entries, user feedback submissions.
  • Third-party OAuth tokens: access and refresh tokens and associated athlete IDs for Garmin Connect, Strava and COROS. Tokens are stored locally in the PACR database and are never shared.
  • Location data: optional city/address entries, GPS tracks from activities, geocoding requests via Nominatim (OpenStreetMap), and map rendering via Leaflet / OSM tiles.
  • Log and access data: IP address, user agent, timestamp and requested URL (for operation, security and abuse prevention).
  • Audit log: security-relevant actions such as login, password change or FIT upload (stored via the internal AuditLogService).

Note: PACR currently uses no payment provider. Once a subscription payment flow (e.g. Stripe) is launched, this section and Section 6 will be updated accordingly beforehand.

5. Garmin Connect Integration

When you connect your Garmin account to PACR, we access data from the Garmin Connect Developer APIs via OAuth 2.0. The connection is established only upon your explicit consent.

  • Data retrieved: completed activities including metrics (time, distance, pace, heart rate, power, cadence, elevation, GPS); and – where authorised – daily summaries, heart-rate, sleep and HRV data.
  • Purpose: automatic synchronisation of your training sessions into PACR, training analysis, plan generation and progress tracking.
  • Storage: retrieved activity data is stored on our server infrastructure in the EU (tables completed_trainings and activity_streams). OAuth tokens are stored securely in the backend.
  • No sharing: Garmin data is not sold or shared with third parties. It is used exclusively for your personal training analysis.
  • Disconnecting: you can revoke the Garmin connection at any time within PACR under Profile → Integrations → Disconnect Garmin. The OAuth token is then deleted and further synchronisation stops.
  • Data deletion: upon request to info@pacr.app we delete all data imported from Garmin. Alternatively you can revoke the authorisation directly in your Garmin Connect account.
  • Current status: manual FIT-file processing is actively implemented. Automatic OAuth synchronisation will be enabled after approval by the Garmin Connect Developer Program.

6. Other Third-Party Integrations

To operate PACR we rely on the following external service providers. Any transfers to third countries are made on the basis of appropriate safeguards pursuant to Art. 44 et seq. GDPR (such as EU standard contractual clauses).

  • Strava (Strava Inc., USA) – OAuth 2.0, scope activity:read_all. Activities including streams are retrieved. Disconnect under Profile → Integrations → Disconnect Strava.
  • COROS (Coros Wearables Inc.) – OAuth via open.coros.com, including webhook for activity push. Disconnect under Profile → Integrations → Disconnect COROS.
  • OpenAI (OpenAI, L.L.C., USA) – integrated via LangChain4j for AI-assisted analysis (model: gpt-4o-mini). For analyses, selected training data or descriptions are transmitted as prompt text. Used only when AI features are actively invoked.
  • Nominatim (OpenStreetMap Foundation, UK) – geocoding for address lookups in the location picker. The search query and your IP address are transmitted.
  • Leaflet & OpenStreetMap tiles – map rendering inside the application; your browser loads map tiles directly from the tile server (IP is transmitted).
  • Google Fonts (Google Ireland Ltd.) – loading of the Lexend font from fonts.googleapis.com (IP is transmitted).
  • Tailwind CDN (Vercel/Cloudflare) – delivery of the CSS framework on the landing page (IP is transmitted).
  • Microsoft 365 / Outlook (Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd.) – sending of transactional e-mails (registration confirmation, password reset) via SMTP.

7. Hosting and Infrastructure

PACR is operated on server infrastructure located within the European Union. Application data is stored in a MariaDB database; uploaded files are stored on the application server. The connection between browser and server is always encrypted via HTTPS/TLS.

The concrete hosting provider and the data-centre location can be provided on request to info@pacr.app. A data processing agreement pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR is in place with the hosting provider.

8. Retention Periods

  • Account data is kept until you delete your account. Deletion is performed completely and cascaded via the internal UserDeletionService.
  • Training and activity data is retained until you delete it individually or delete your account.
  • OAuth tokens (Garmin, Strava, COROS) are removed as soon as you disconnect the corresponding service.
  • Log and access data is generally deleted after no more than 90 days unless it is needed to investigate a specific security incident.
  • Audit-log entries are retained longer for security reasons, but at most until the associated account is deleted.

9. International Data Transfers

Some of the providers listed in Section 6 (in particular OpenAI, Strava, Google Fonts and Tailwind CDN) are based outside the EU/EEA, primarily in the United States. Such transfers are carried out on the basis of appropriate safeguards under Art. 44 et seq. GDPR – in particular EU standard contractual clauses and, where applicable, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework.

10. Security

PACR implements technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including:

  • encrypted transmission via HTTPS/TLS,
  • passwords stored exclusively as BCrypt hashes,
  • authentication via short-lived JWT tokens with refresh-token rotation,
  • server-side ownership checks on all user-scoped API endpoints,
  • role-based access control (Spring Security),
  • a separate production environment with secrets provided only via environment variables.

11. Your Rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights: access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17), restriction of processing (Art. 18), data portability (Art. 20) and objection (Art. 21). You may withdraw any consent given with effect for the future at any time. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority – in Germany this is the data-protection authority responsible for your place of residence.

How to exercise your rights in PACR:

  • Delete your account entirely: in the app under Profile → Delete account. Your account and all associated training and activity data are removed in a cascading deletion process.
  • Disconnect Garmin, Strava or COROS: in the app under Profile → Integrations.
  • Request subject access or data export: by e-mail to info@pacr.app.
  • Withdraw consent (e.g. for AI analysis): directly in the app or by e-mail to info@pacr.app.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This privacy policy is updated whenever there are material changes to the scope of the service or to the providers used. The current version is always available at pacr.app/en/privacy.html. The date of the latest update is noted at the top of this document.
